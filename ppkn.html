National Palace Museum

Explore the rich history and culture of Taiwan through the treasures housed in the National Palace Museum.

About the Museum

The National Palace Museum is one of the most renowned museums in the world, showcasing a vast collection of Chinese imperial artifacts and artworks. It is located in Taipei, Taiwan, and serves as a cultural bridge between the past and present, offering visitors a glimpse into the rich heritage of Chinese civilization.

Interesting Facts

Over 700,000 Artifacts

The museum houses over 700,000 pieces of ancient Chinese artifacts, making it one of the largest collections in the world.

Established in 1925

Originally established in Beijing, the museum was relocated to Taiwan in 1949 during the Chinese Civil War.

International Recognition

The museum is recognized globally for its extensive collection and is a popular destination for tourists and scholars alike.

Educational Programs

The museum offers various educational programs and workshops to promote understanding of Chinese culture and history.
